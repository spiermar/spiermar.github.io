- most guardrails are trying to solve the wrong problem
- the goal is not to slow down developers locally, the goal is to protect production and limit blast radius
- `codex --yolo` is fine when the system is designed to absorb mistakes safely
- antifragile architecture matters more than restrictive local workflows
- small failures in development and rollout should make the system better, not create organizational panic
- the real guardrails are safe rollout processes, progressive exposure, and fast recovery paths
- development sandboxes are what make aggressive agentic workflows viable
- I can go deeper later on rollout practices, development practices, and resiliency architecture patterns
- getting systems, processes, and teams to this level takes a lot of effort
- that effort will widen the gap between strong companies and weak companies even more
