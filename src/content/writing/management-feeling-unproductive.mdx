- two things that made me feel unproductive
- backlog of things to do
  - as an IC, I could get to the end of the day, and have finished everything I had to do, or at least, what I planned to do
  - as a manager, that will never happen. the amount of things you are responsible for, and the amount of unplanned work that arrises every day is too great for a single person to deal with (that's why you have a team)
  - plus, you can always go deeper and get into more details, improve things
  - so I frequently felt unproductive, because I could never plan and accomplish everything I had planned in a given time frame
  - I could never get to the end of the day, and feel like I was done
  - it's a constant work of priorization. you will focus on the important things first, and inevitably, a few things won't make the cut
  - and that's fine
  - but it took me a long time to figure that one out
- time spent in meetings
  - always felt that I couldn't get anything done, because I spent most of my day in meetings
  - it took me a long time to be in peace with the fact that this was the job
  - be in meetings, giving direction, alingning, making decisions
  - and that it was my job to delegate the hands on job to the team
